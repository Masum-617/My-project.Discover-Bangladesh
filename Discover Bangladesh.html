Top Tourist Destinations

Cox's Bazar

The longest sea beach in the world, stretching 120 km along the Bay of Bengal.

Saint-martins-island

Saint Martin's Island is the only coral island in Bangladesh. Locally known as Narikel Jinjira or Coconut Island.

Sundarbans

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, home to the Bengal tiger and rich biodiversity.

Kaptai-Lake

Kaptai Lake is the largest man-made lake in Bangladesh. Aerial view of islands in Kaptai Lake in Rangamati of Chittagong in Bangladesh.

Boga-lake

BogaLake is a wonderful natural lake known for its scenic beauty, 65km from Bandarban and 20 km from Ruma Thana.

Srimangal

Srimongal is the place of tea gardens in Sylhet, hills and forest areas in Bangladesh. Srimangal is famous for the largest tea gardens of the world.

Cultural Heritage of Bangladesh

Historical Sites

Bangladesh is home to a rich history with ancient temples, mosques, and historical monuments like the Sixty Dome Mosque, Paharpur Buddhist Vihara, and Lalbagh Fort. Each site tells the story of the nation's diverse cultural influences over the centuries.

Festivals and Traditions

From the Bengali New Year (Pohela Boishakh) to Durga Puja, Bangladesh celebrates vibrant festivals. These reflect the deep-rooted traditions and the harmony of different communities across the country.
